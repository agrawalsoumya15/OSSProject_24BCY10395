1. Origin Story of VLC Media Player 
1. Who made VLC 
VLC Media Player was created by a group of students in France. These 
students were part of an organization called VideoLAN. The project started 
around the year 1996. It was not made by a big company. Instead, it was 
made by students who were learning and experimenting with networking and 
media streaming. 
At that time, these students were studying in a university and working on a 
project related to video streaming. Their goal was to send video across a 
network inside their campus. Since they did not have a proper tool to do this, 
they decided to build their own software. 
Later, this project grew bigger. More developers from different parts of the 
world joined it. It slowly became a global open-source project instead of just 
a student project. 
2. Why they made VLC 
The main reason for creating VLC was to solve a practical problem. The 
students wanted to stream video from one system to another inside their 
university network. But at that time, there were no good tools available that 
were free and flexible. 
Most of the existing media players were limited. They could not handle 
different types of video formats properly. Also, many of them were not 
designed for network streaming. Some tools were available, but they were 
either too expensive or too restricted. 
So, instead of depending on these tools, the students decided to create their 
own media player. They wanted something that could: 
• Play different types of video and audio files  
• Work over a network  
• Be flexible and customizable  
This is how VLC was born. At first, it was only used inside the university. But 
later, people realized its usefulness, and it was shared with the public. 
3. What problem existed before VLC 
Before VLC, there were many problems related to media playback. 
First, most media players could not support many formats. If you had a video 
file in a different format, the player would simply not play it. Users had to 
install extra codecs again and again, which was confusing and time
consuming. 
Second, streaming video over a network was not easy. There were very few

tools that allowed smooth streaming, especially in a local network like a 
university campus. 
Third, many media players were proprietary. This means their source code 
was not available. Because of this, users could not modify the software or fix 
problems on their own. They had to depend completely on the company. 
Also, some software required payment or licenses, which was not affordable 
for students. 
All these problems made it clear that there was a need for a better solution 
— something simple, free, and powerful. 
4. Why they made it open source 
The creators of VLC decided to make it open source because they believed 
in sharing knowledge and helping others. 
Since they were students themselves, they understood the importance of 
free tools. They wanted other students and developers to use the software 
without any cost. Making it open source allowed anyone to download, use, 
and improve the software. 
Another reason was collaboration. When a project is open source, 
developers from around the world can contribute to it. This helps the 
software grow faster and become better over time. VLC improved a lot 
because many people added new features, fixed bugs, and supported new 
formats. 
Open source also gives freedom. Users can study how the software works, 
modify it according to their needs, and even share their own versions. This 
was not possible with proprietary software. 
By making VLC open source, the developers ensured that it would remain 
free and accessible to everyone. Today, VLC is used all over the world 
because of this decision.
